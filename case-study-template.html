The Challenge

When this home decor brand approached me, they were struggling with flat organic traffic despite having a beautiful website and quality products. Their organic channel was generating less than 15% of total revenue, and they were heavily dependent on paid advertising.

The main issues I identified during my initial audit:

  • Poor site architecture making it difficult for search engines to crawl and index product pages efficiently
  • Thin product descriptions with no unique value proposition or keyword optimization
  • No content strategy to capture top-of-funnel searches and build topical authority
  • Technical SEO issues including slow page speed, duplicate content, and missing structured data
  • Weak internal linking leaving valuable pages orphaned with no link equity flowing to them

My Approach

I developed a comprehensive SEO strategy focused on building a solid technical foundation, creating valuable content, and establishing the brand as an authority in the home decor space.

  • Technical Foundation: Fixed crawlability issues, improved Core Web Vitals scores, implemented proper schema markup, and resolved duplicate content problems
  • Keyword Research: Identified 500+ high-intent keywords across the buyer journey, from informational queries to product-specific searches
  • Content Strategy: Created a content calendar with buying guides, style inspiration articles, and product comparison content to capture searchers at every stage
  • Product Page Optimization: Rewrote product descriptions with unique, keyword-rich content that highlights benefits and answers common questions
  • Internal Linking: Built a strategic internal linking structure that distributed authority to key product and category pages

Key Takeaways

This project reinforced several important principles that I apply to all my e-commerce SEO work:

  • Technical SEO is the foundation. Without a crawlable, fast website, content optimization efforts will underperform. Always start with the technical basics.
  • Content builds authority. Product pages alone cannot compete for informational queries. A robust content strategy captures traffic at every stage of the buyer journey.
  • Patience pays off. SEO takes time, but the results compound. The traffic and revenue gains from this project continue to grow months after the initial work.
  • Data drives decisions. Regular analysis and willingness to adjust strategy based on what the data shows is crucial for sustained success.
