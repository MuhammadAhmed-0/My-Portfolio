The Story

I worked with a high-average-order-value (AOV) e-commerce store selling premium products. The client was running Facebook Ads but struggled to achieve sustainable growth and profitable scaling. They needed a strategy that could improve performance while allowing them to confidently increase ad spend.

Client Challenge

The biggest challenge was cost control at scale. Cost per purchase was high, and whenever the client tried to increase budget, performance dropped sharply. Campaign results were inconsistent, making it difficult to predict revenue or plan long-term growth.

My Strategy

To solve these issues, I implemented a data-driven Facebook Ads strategy focused on scalability, audience precision, and creative testing:

  • Existing Campaign Audit: Identified inefficiencies and budget leaks.
  • Audience Segmentation: Split campaigns into multiple audience segments.
  • Custom & Lookalike Audiences: Built audiences based on buyer and engagement data.
  • Creative Diversification: Used video, carousel, and image ads.
  • Messaging Tests: Tested different value propositions and product angles.
  • Tiered Campaign Structure: Allocated budget based on performance tiers.
  • Budget Caps: Controlled spend to protect profitability.
  • Dynamic Retargeting: Re-engaged warm users with tailored messaging.
  • Upsell Campaigns: Increased customer lifetime value.
  • Scaling Winners: Gradually scaled best-performing ad sets.

The Results

The campaign delivered strong and sustainable improvements across key performance metrics:

  • 39% increase in purchases
  • 17% reduction in cost per purchase
  • Consistent and predictable sales growth

By refining targeting, improving creatives, and implementing structured scaling, the client was able to grow revenue while maintaining profitability.

"Scaling was always a problem for us because costs would rise too fast. After restructuring the campaigns and improving targeting, results became much more stable. We were finally able to scale our ad spend without sacrificing profitability."
